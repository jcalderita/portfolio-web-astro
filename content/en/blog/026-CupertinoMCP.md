## 💡 Cupertino MCP: The Solution

**[Cupertino MCP](https://github.com/mihaelamj/cupertino)** is a tool that <span class="high">locally indexes all of Apple's documentation</span> and makes it available to your AI through the **Model Context Protocol (MCP)**.

In plain English: it's like giving your AI (Claude, in my case) a **direct, verified gateway** to all of Apple's official documentation 🎯.

**How much documentation are we talking about?**

📄 **302,424+ pages** of official documentation<br />
🧰 **307 frameworks** indexed<br />
📦 **9,699 Swift packages** cataloged<br />
💾 **606 sample code projects** from Apple<br />
📱 Complete **Human Interface Guidelines**<br />
📖 **Swift Evolution proposals** (~400 proposals)<br />
🏛️ **Apple Archive guides** (legacy but valuable documentation)

All of this, **available offline**, no internet needed, and with <span class="high">zero risk of AI hallucinations</span> 🛡️.

## 📊 Super Powerful Search

Cupertino uses **SQLite FTS5 with BM25 ranking**. In plain English: <span class="high">ultra-fast</span> searches (under 100ms) with relevant results sorted by importance.

You can filter by:
- Specific framework 🧰
- Platform version (iOS 17, macOS 14, etc.) 📱
- Documentation type (API, examples, guides) 📚
- Sample code search 💻
